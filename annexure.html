Stepwise protocol

Step I: cDNA Synthesis

  1. Thaw RNA (samples) on ice. 
  2. Aliquot 11 µl RNA from each samples into a 96 well plate.
  3. Add 2.75 μl of Luna Script RT supermix to each well. It is recommended to perform this step while placing the 96-well plate on ice. 
  4. Mix by Pipetting and seal the plate. Give a short spin to the plate.   
  5. Place the plate in a thermal cycler with the following conditions. The thermal cycler should be pre-set on 25° C.

Step III: Purification of PCR product 

  1. Bring the AXP beads and Qubit reagents at room temperature. Make sure AXP beads are at room temperature before its use to avoid the clump formation. 
  2. Briefly spin the plate label with pool 1 and pool 2. 
  3. Carefully pool the pool 1 and pool 2 together for each sample. Avoid any cross mixing/contamination while pooling. 
  4. Give a short spin to the plate containing pooled PCR product. Measure the volume of pooled PCR product in each well. 
  5. Vortex the AXP beads. 
  6. Add equal volume of AXP beads to the each well. 
  7. Seal the plate tightly and vortex for 10 min (rpm 700 to 900).
  8. Spin down the plate and gently remove the seal. Place the plate on a magnetic rack. Wait for 2-5 min for the separation of the beads. Observe for the clear and colourless supernatant. 
  9. Using a multichannel pipette, carefully discard the supernatant. 
  10. Wash the AXP beads pellet by adding 200 µl freshly prepared 80% ethanol, wait 30 seconds. Using a multichannel pipette, remove the ethanol without disturbing the pellet.
  11. Repeat the ethanol wash of the pellet as per step 10. 
  12. Remove any residual ethanol drops using 10 µl pipette. Allow to air dry the pellet for 2 to 5 min.
  13. Remove the plate from a magnetic rack and resuspend the pellet in 16 µl of nuclease free water. 
  14. Incubate the plate for 2 min at RT. 
  15. Briefly spin down the plate and keep it at a magnetic rack for 2 min. Elute 15 µl of clear supernatant in a fresh plate. 
  16. Quantify the purified PCR product using Qubit. (Nanodrop is not recommended)

Step V: Barcode Ligation, Pooling and clean up

  1. Take a fresh 96-well plate and label as barcode-ligation plate. 
  2. Set the barcode ligation reaction by combining the reaction component as mentioned below:

Component

Volume (µl)

End-prepped DNA

7.5

Native Barcode

2.5

2X Blunt TA ligase master mix

10

Final volume of mix

20.0
  1. Thoroughly mix the reaction by gently pipetting.   Seal the plate and give a short spin. 
  2. Incubate the plate at RT for 20 min.  
  3. Thaw EDTA, AXP beads and SFB at RT. Prepare 80 % Ethanol. 
  4. Followed by the incubation of 20 min, add 2 µl EDTA (from clear cap EDTA-vial) or 4 µl EDTA (from blue cap EDTA-vail) to each well. Mix thoroughly by pipetting and spin down briefly.
  5. Resuspend the AXP beads by vortexing. Add 9 µl of AXP beads (if EDTA was added from clear cap vail) or 10 µl of AXP beads (if EDTA was added from blue cap vail) to the each well. Mix well using the pipette. 
  6. Pool the content from all the wells in a 1.5 ml centrifuge tube (Eppendorf DNA LoBind tube is recommended).   
  7. Incubate on a Hula mixer for 10 min at RT (35 rpm). Intermittently, flick the tubes to ensure the better mixing of the content. 
  8. Spin down the sample and place the tube on a magnetic stand for 5 min. Observe for the clear and colourless supernatant. Using the pipette, discard the supernatant without disturbing the pellet.
  9. To wash the pellet, add 700 µl of freshly prepared 80 % ethanol without disturbing the pellet. To avoid any disturbance to the pellet, add ethanol slowly through the wall of the tube, away from the pellet.  Remove the ethanol using the pipette and discard. 
  10. Repeat the ethanol wash as per step 11. 
  11. Spin down briefly and place the tube back on a magnetic stand. Remove any residual ethanol. Allow the pellet to dry for 30 secs to 1 min. Do not let the pellet to crack. 
  12. Remove the tube from the magnetic stand and add 35 µl of nuclease free water on the pellet. Resuspend the pellet by gently flicking. 
  13. Incubate for 10 min at 37°C. At every 2 min, gently flicking the tube is recommended for the enhance DNA elution. 
  14. Place the tube on a magnetic stand until the eluate is clear and colourless (Approx. 2 min). 
  15. Collect 35µL of eluate in a fresh 1.5 ml micro centrifuge tube.   
  16. Measure the concentration of DNA in the eluate using Qubit.

  1. Mix the reaction by gently pipetting. Spin down briefly.  
  2. Incubate for 20 min at RT.
  3. Vortex AXP beads. 
  4. Add (0.6x volume) 30 µL of AXP beads and mix by pipetting.
  5. Incubate the tube on Hula mixer for 10 min at RT. 
  6. Spin down briefly and place the tube on a magnetic stand. Observe for the clear supernatant. Pipette off the clear supernatant. 
  7. Wash the beads using SFB. Add 125 µl SFB to the beads. Flick the beads to resuspend, briefly spin down and place the tube on a magnetic stand and allow the beads to pellet. Remove the clear supernatant through pipette.  
  8. Repeat the SFB wash of the beads as per the step 8.  
  9. Briefly spin down and place the tube on magnetic stand. Remove any residual supernatant. 
  10. Resuspend the pellet in 15 µl of elution buffer. Spin down and incubate for 10 min at 37°C. At every 2 min, gently flicking the tube is recommended for the enhance DNA elution. 
  11. Place the tube on a magnetic stand until the eluate is clear and colourless (Approx. 2 min). 
  12. Collect 15 µL of eluate in a fresh 1.5 ml micro centrifuge tube.    
  13. Measure the concentration of DNA in the eluate using Qubit.

Step VII: Priming and loading the SpoTON flow cell

  1. Take out the flow cell from 4 °C at least 30 min before the flow cell priming and thaw at RT.
  2. Set the flow cell on the sequencing device (MK1c or GridION). Perform the flow cell QC and check the number of pores in the flow cell before its priming. 
  3. Prepare flow cell priming mix in a fresh 1.5 ml tube as follow:  

Reagent

Volume (µl)

Flush buffer (FCF)

1170

Flush tether (FCT)

30

Final volume of mix

1200
  1. Vortex and spin down the priming mix.
  2. Open the priming port.
  3. After opening the priming port, check for a small air bubble under the cover. Draw back a small volume to remove any bubbles:
    1. Set a P1000 pipette to 200 µl.
    2. Insert the tip into the priming port.
    3. Turn the wheel until the dial shows 220-230 µl, to draw back 20-30 ul, or until you can see a small volume of buffer entering the pipette tip.

Note: Visually check that there is continuous buffer from the priming port across the sensor array.

  1. Load 800 µl of the priming mix into the flow cell via the priming port, avoid introducing air bubbles. Wait for 5 min before second priming. Utilize this incubation period to prepare the library-loading mix by combining following components:

  1. Gently lift the SpotON sample port cover to make the SpotON sample port accessible.
  2. Load 200 µl of the priming mix into the flow cell priming port (make sure SpotON is open while doing second priming).  Avoid introducing air bubbles.
  3. Mix thoroughly library-loading mix by gently pipetting up and down just prior to loading.
  4. Add 75 μl of the library-loading mix to the flow cell via the SpotON port (sample port) in a dropwise fashion. Ensure each drop flows into the port before adding the next.
  5. Gently replace the SpotON port cover, making sure the bung enters the SpotON port, close the priming port and replace the MinION device lid.
  6. Start the sequencing.

Step VIII: Washing of the Flow cell

  1. Once the sequencing is complete, flow cell may be washed to reuse it for next sequencing run.
  2. Thaw DIL, WMX and storage solutions. Make a flow cell wash mix by adding 2 µl of WMX to the 398 µl of DIL. Mix thoroughly by pipetting. Spin down briefly.
  3. While SpotON and priming ports remain closed, slowly pipette off the content of flow cell through waste port and discard it. 
  4. As mentioned earlier (point 6 of step VII), checked for the small air bubble from priming port. 
  5. Through priming port slowly add the flow cell wash mix and incubate the flow cell at RT for 1 h. 
  6. Followed by the incubation of 1 h, remove the content through waste port of the flow cell.
  7. Again, do the bubble check (point 6 of step VII)
  8. Through priming port, add 500 µl of storage solution. Perform the QC of flow cell and store it for reuse in next run.
